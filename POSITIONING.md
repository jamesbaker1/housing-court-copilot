## Positioning: "Legal Aid Intake Autopilot for Eviction Defense"

**The wedge.** Housing Court Copilot is a tenant-facing workflow engine that makes each tenant *easy for a legal-services org to help*. It classifies the situation, explains the law generally, builds a checklist, gathers (tenant-verified) evidence from open data, transcribes the tenant's own factual narrative into **factual** answer fields (scrivener-adjacent — see the UPL caveat in Risks), packages a clean consented file, and routes to a human attorney. It is conservative on legal conclusions and ends in a human handoff. It is **not** an "AI lawyer for tenants."

> **Foundational dependency (Phase-0 gate).** Two relationships must be in place *before* any fact-classification or defense-checklist output ships: (1) a named **design-partner provider** that has agreed the provider-side ROI metrics below actually matter to them and has defined "complete / consented / categorized = accepted without re-work"; and (2) a **supervising attorney in the loop** who reviews work product. The UPL posture (and the "selection of facts" exposure flagged in Risks) depends on the attorney relationship existing at launch, not arriving in a later phase. Without a signed provider, the core proof metric is self-defined and unfalsifiable.

### Why "intake autopilot" beats "AI lawyer"

1. **It attacks the real bottleneck (capacity, not demand).** NYC full-representation rates fell sharply post-pandemic — the Comptroller's May 2025 report supports roughly **71% (FY21) declining to ~42% (FY24)** ([Comptroller](https://comptroller.nyc.gov/reports/evictions-up-representation-down/)). *[Flag: the further "~30% by early 2025" point and the "Q4 FY" quarter labels are over-precise and not cleanly pinpointed in the cited report — re-source before using in any external materials.]* Eligible demand rose substantially while funding rose far less ([City Limits](https://citylimits.org/fewer-eligible-tenants-get-right-to-counsel-after-pandemic-program-expansion-report/)). *[Flag: the prior "demand tripled (~222%) / funding +129%" pairing was not the figure in the cited Comptroller/IBO sources, which point to eligibility roughly doubling (~110% increase 2022–2024) and RTC funding up on the order of ~33%. Use the doubling/+33% framing and re-verify against the primary report before publication.]* Providers are strained on attorney-hours (high attrition, rising hours/case, caseloads well above recommended levels) *[Flag: the specific "attrition 20–55%/yr, hours/case +24% since 2018, caseloads 50–80 vs ~48 recommended" figures are presented without citation and are the quantitative backbone of the capacity thesis — each must be individually sourced or dropped.]* An autopilot that cuts non-billable screening time *multiplies* provider capacity; an "AI lawyer" that replaces the lawyer competes with — and forgoes the proven gains of — the funded RTC system.

2. **Lower UPL risk — with an important caveat.** In NY, applying law to a specific tenant's facts ("which defense to raise," "do you have a case") is regulated advice under [Jud. Law §§478/484](https://law.justia.com/codes/new-york/jud/article-15/478/). **[Upsolve v. James](https://law.justia.com/cases/federal/appellate-courts/ca2/22-1345/22-1345-2025-09-09.html)** (2d Cir., Sept. 9 2025) is frequently mis-cited as settling that free individualized help is UPL; it did **not**. The Second Circuit **vacated** the district court's preliminary injunction and **remanded**, holding NY's UPL rules are content-neutral and subject to **intermediate** (not strict) scrutiny — it did *not* finally adjudicate that Upsolve's conduct is UPL, and the merits return to the district court. A cert petition (with the Institute for Justice) was filed ~Feb. 2026. **The legal posture is unresolved**; do not treat it as settled. Separately, the **scrivener / "verbatim transcription" safe harbor is narrow** — it covers transcribing a person's *own* words, and an LLM that re-phrases, selects, or flags which open-data violations are "repair-defense evidence" is arguably *selecting legally-relevant facts*, which lives close to the advice line (see Risks). Intake, eligibility screening, reminders, faithful form-fill, packet assembly, and warm referral sit on the **legal-information/clerical** side — the same envelope NY's own CourtHelp / Court Navigators operate in — but the "organize/select facts" steps need attorney review, not just a "should I…" classifier.

3. **More fundable — but the LSC-TIG path has a structural hole that must be reconciled.** LSC Technology Initiative Grants explicitly fund **intake modernization, eligibility triage, smart referral, and AI-streamlined intake — not "AI lawyers"** ([LSC 2024 TIG awards](https://www.lsc.gov/press-release/lsc-awards-59-million-technology-grants-31-civil-legal-aid-providers)). **However:** (a) the only LSC grantee in NYC is **Legal Services NYC** — most NYC RTC providers (Legal Aid Society, NYLAG, Bronx Defenders, Mobilization for Justice, TakeRoot, CAMBA, etc.) are **not** LSC-funded and cannot receive TIG money; and (b) LSC grantees are **barred from serving undocumented immigrants** (program-integrity rule applies even to non-LSC funds), yet a large share of NYC eviction-defense tenants are undocumented. An LSC-TIG-funded build co-applying with Legal Services NYC would therefore be structurally unable to serve much of the intended population through that partner. **Resolution required:** either (i) scope LSC-TIG to the documented/eligible subset and fund the broader build through **non-LSC sources** (NYC/State A2J funds, IOLA/NYBF, private/philanthropic, City OCJ), partnering with non-LSC RTC providers for the undocumented-inclusive service; or (ii) treat LSC-TIG as one funding leg among several, not the spine. Do not present "co-apply for LSC TIG with an RTC provider" as the funding strategy without naming which provider and which population it covers.

4. **It helps both sides.** Tenants get appearance, navigation, and a complete file; providers get pre-screened, pre-assembled, consented, well-categorized referrals that raise effective capacity — *if* a design-partner provider has agreed to accept them.

5. **It captures the dominant loss mechanism — framed carefully.** A large share of tenants lose **by default**, and not appearing is a major driver ([Furman Center, "Half the Battle is Just Showing Up"](https://furmancenter.org/research/publication/half-the-battle-is-just-showing-up)). *[Flag: the prior "30–50% lose by default for not showing up" and "~92% of defaults at the first appearance" figures conflate non-answer with failure-to-appear (distinct concepts) and the 92% statistic is not evident in the cited Furman brief — drop or re-source the 92% figure and separate non-answer from FTA.]* Reminders + navigation + assembly + handoff — no legal advice required — still target this loss mechanism.

6. **Avoid the deception trap.** The FTC's **[DoNotPay order](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2025/02/ftc-finalizes-order-donotpay-prohibits-deceptive-ai-lawyer-claims-imposes-monetary-relief-requires)** (Feb 2025, $193k) makes "robot lawyer / substitute for a lawyer" marketing independently actionable under FTC §5 — a federal red line distinct from UPL.

### Proof metrics (what we measure — not "cases won by AI")

- **Capacity multiplier:** staff-minutes-per-intake ↓ / intakes-completed-per-attorney-hour ↑ — *measurable only with the design-partner provider's cooperation; define the baseline jointly.*
- **Referral completeness:** % of handoffs arriving complete, consented, and categorized — accepted by the provider without re-work (the partner must define "without re-work").
- **Appearance / non-default rate** among assisted tenants. *[Flag: do not import specific FTA-reduction magnitudes (e.g., "12–53%," "ideas42 ~36%") from the criminal-summons RCT literature — those studies are in the criminal-summons context and the external-validity transfer to Housing Court eviction appearances is unsupported. Measure the effect in-context rather than asserting a borrowed magnitude.]*
- **Representation-uptake rate:** share of triaged tenants who reach a funded RTC lawyer with a usable file.
- **Assistance applications:** rental/cash-assistance completion. *[Measurement caveat: because ACCESS HRA / One Shot Deal / CityFHEPS have no submission API, "completion" and "approval" are **not directly observable by the tool** — they require tenant self-report or provider data-sharing. Treat this as a self-report/partner-data metric, not a system-captured one, or down-rank it.]*

These tie to provider/funder ROI — and steer clear of adversarial "AI-won-the-case" framing that invites UPL scrutiny.
