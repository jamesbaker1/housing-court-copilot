## Risks & Red Lines

**1. Unauthorized Practice of Law (UPL) — the dominant risk, with the *Upsolve* posture corrected.** NY [Jud. Law §§478/484](https://law.justia.com/codes/new-york/jud/article-15/478/) criminalize nonlawyer legal advice (misdemeanor). **[Upsolve v. James](https://law.justia.com/cases/federal/appellate-courts/ca2/22-1345/22-1345-2025-09-09.html)** (2d Cir., Sept. 9 2025) is *not* settled UPL law: the Second Circuit **vacated** the district court's preliminary injunction and **remanded**, holding NY's UPL rules are content-neutral and subject to **intermediate** scrutiny — it did **not** finally adjudicate that Upsolve's free, individualized help is UPL; the merits return to the district court, and a cert petition (with the Institute for Justice) was filed ~Feb. 2026. **Treat the law as unresolved**, not as confirming that "check-this-box" help is UPL.
> **Red lines:** Never tell a specific user which defense to raise, whether they have a valid claim, how to answer their specific complaint, or predict outcome. Stay on the legal-information / faithful-transcription / assembly / referral side, mirroring CourtHelp / Court Navigators. Build a classifier that detects advice-seeking turns ("should I…", "do I have a case", "which defense") and hard-routes them to a human referral. **The reviewing attorney must be engaged from Phase 0** — the UPL posture depends on it.

**1a. The scrivener "verbatim transcription" safe harbor is narrow — and "selection of facts" is the real exposure.** NY's scrivener exception covers transcribing a person's *own* words. An LLM that re-phrases, organizes, *selects*, or flags which open-data violations count as "repair-defense evidence" is arguably doing more than transcription — it is **selecting legally-relevant facts**, which sits exactly on the line between permissible organization and impermissible application of law to facts. The "should I" classifier does **not** address this subtler exposure.
> **Red lines:** Keep the model's fact-handling to faithful transcription of the tenant's own narrative; do not let the tool *characterize* which facts are legally favorable. Any "this looks like a registration/repair/Good Cause issue" framing must be (a) clearly information-not-advice, and (b) attorney-reviewed before it reaches the tenant or a filing. Prefer presenting raw, tenant-verified data over tool-generated legal salience.

**2. S7263 — chatbot "proprietor" civil liability targets a tool like this directly (elevated to top-tier).** S7263/S7263A (Sen. Gonzalez) imposes **civil liability on the chatbot "proprietor" (owner/operator/deployer)** for outputs amounting to legal advice — it points **directly at this product**, not merely at foundation-model vendors, and **passed the Senate Internet & Technology Committee in early March 2026**. If enacted it creates a **private right of action** for any output a court deems legal advice — a potentially product-killing, product-specific risk (not a peripheral "would tighten UPL" footnote).
> **Red lines:** Architect from day one so no output can be characterized as individualized legal advice; preserve attorney-in-the-loop accountability; monitor S7263's progress as a gating legal event; obtain NY counsel's read before any feature that interprets a specific tenant's situation.

**3. "AI lawyer" marketing (FTC §5).** The **[DoNotPay order](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2025/02/ftc-finalizes-order-donotpay-prohibits-deceptive-ai-lawyer-claims-imposes-monetary-relief-requires)** ($193k, Feb 2025) makes "robot lawyer / substitute for a lawyer" claims independently actionable.
> **Red line:** Never market as a lawyer, legal advice, or attorney substitute. Substantiate any accuracy/outcome claim. Persistent in-product "legal information, not legal advice; not a lawyer" disclaimer.

**4. Court-data scraping / ToS.** The live UCS eCourts/WebCivil/eTrack portals are Cloudflare + CAPTCHA protected, are a free *individual-lookup* service with **no bulk/scrape license**, and the UCS ToS prohibits bots/spiders/crawlers — raising ToS and potential CFAA/contract exposure.
> **Red lines:** Do not scrape the live court portal at scale. Get court data via OCA's formal data-request process, published Open Data / OCA deidentified extracts, **eTrack email ingest** (sanctioned), and **NYSCEF public docket** for e-filed cases. **Never ingest sealed records** (CPL 160.50).

**5. Tool-generated factual assertions in a court filing — stale open data + filer liability (NEW).** Open data (HPD/DOB) lags, has mismatched BBLs, and shows closed violations as open. The **tenant is the filer** and bears the credibility/sanctions risk under **22 NYCRR 130** (and the *Mata v. Avianca* / Dennis Block line on AI-generated filings) if any auto-asserted fact is wrong. A tool-built "violation timeline" the tenant files could *cause* the harm the tool exists to prevent.
> **Red lines:** Every open-data-derived assertion carries a **data-accuracy disclaimer** and passes a **tenant "verify before filing" gate**; never auto-assert a factual claim into a filing without human review; prefer raw, source-linked data the tenant confirms over tool-asserted conclusions; analyze tool liability for any factual assertion that reaches the court.

**6. Wrong-deadline / wrong-date malpractice-style liability (NEW).** Eviction has hard statutory clocks (e.g., the answer window, OSC timing). A reminder or assembly step that **miscalculates or mis-sends a deadline can directly cause a default** — direct causation of the exact harm the tool targets. This is distinct from TCPA/SMS consent risk.
> **Red lines:** Treat date/deadline computation as safety-critical: human confirmation of extracted dates, conservative buffers, no silent automation, and explicit "confirm your court date with the court" prompts. Carry no statutory-deadline calculation as authoritative without attorney-validated logic.

**7. NYC Open Data terms.** Broadly reusable incl. commercial (Local Law 11), no license/registration/usage restriction — but republishers may need to identify source/version/modifications, and users accept NYC.gov ToU/Privacy Policy. JustFix's NYCDB **prebuilt data dumps are CC BY-NC-SA (non-commercial)** and the WoW API is undocumented (and the `/aggregate` endpoint earlier cited does not exist in the current client).
> **Red lines:** For commercial use, load NYCDB-style data from **original NYC open-data sources**, not redistributed non-commercial dumps. Identify source/version on republish. Don't treat the WoW API as a contractual SLA — confirm acceptable use with JustFix and re-verify which endpoints are live.

**8. Sensitive PII privacy (NY SHIELD Act) + FCRA + immigration-enforcement exposure (expanded).** The tool holds immigration status, benefits, and financial data — highest-sensitivity "private information." SHIELD requires a written security program, encryption, minimization, retention limits, role-based access, breach notification. **FCRA is incomplete if only framed as "don't furnish to landlords":** if the tool ingests a tenant's *own* screening report and generates dispute letters or "overcharge/standing" assessments, accuracy-of-adverse-information and re-characterization risk remain — and there is no treatment of NY's own tenant-screening/blacklisting reforms ([NYSBA](https://nysba.org/legalease-the-use-of-tenant-screening-reports-and-tenant-blacklisting/)). **Immigration exposure is underweighted given 2026 federal posture:** holding immigration-status fields and transmitting a "consented file" to a provider creates discovery/subpoena and ICE-exposure risk that SHIELD compliance alone does not address.
> **Red lines:** Encrypt in transit/at rest; **data-minimization by default — do not collect immigration status unless a specific defense requires it**; defined retention + secure deletion; designate a security coordinator; **a documented legal-hold / subpoena-response plan** for any immigration/benefits data held. **Never** assemble/score/furnish tenant data *to landlords* (triggers full CRA obligations); stay strictly consumer-side (help tenants read/dispute their *own* reports). Provider handoff requires **granular, per-recipient, time-limited, voluntary written consent**; immigration/benefits fields opt-in and severable; consent never a precondition of service (USCIS confidentiality rules).

**9. SMS / TCPA consent.** Statutory damages $500–$1,500 **per text, uncapped**. As of 2025: A2P 10DLC registration mandatory (unregistered traffic blocked), one-to-one consent, opt-out honored by any reasonable method within 10 business days. Regulatory landscape is unsettled (11th/5th Cir. vacated rules 2025–26).
> **Red lines:** Separate, logged SMS opt-in distinct from account signup; keep informational reminders separate from any marketing; honor STOP; register the campaign. Get current legal review before launch.

**10. The attorney-supervision / non-lawyer tension is unresolved (NEW).** ABA Formal Op. 512 governs **licensed lawyers'** use of GenAI — the tool cannot claim its protective umbrella *and* position as non-legal intake (to dodge UPL) **unless a supervising attorney is genuinely in the loop and accountable.** That reintroduces exactly the attorney dependency the wedge tries to minimize.
> **Resolution:** Embrace the dependency rather than paper over it — a real supervising-attorney relationship (Phase-0) is what makes both the UPL posture *and* the Op. 512 quality posture coherent. Where there is no attorney in the loop, the tool must stay strictly on the information/clerical side and not generate fact-characterization. Do not cite Op. 512 as cover absent a genuinely accountable attorney.

**11. Partner dependency + fundability tension (expanded).** Structured handoff depends on **N per-provider LegalServer agreements**; the WoW API is undocumented; the Brooklyn pilot, Roxanne, and Scale Justice (Pro Bono Net rebrand) are relationship-driven with slow timelines. **Plus the unreconciled funding/population tension:** only Legal Services NYC is LSC-funded in NYC and LSC grantees cannot serve undocumented tenants (a large share of the population) — so LSC-TIG cannot be the sole spine.
> **Mitigations:** Always ship a CMS-agnostic PDF-packet fallback (works with zero partner buy-in); self-host the data layer (NYCDB) and assembly engine (docassemble); sequence partnership outreach into **Phase 0**; decide the funding model explicitly (LSC subset + non-LSC funding/partners for the undocumented-inclusive build) rather than assuming a single LSC-TIG co-application covers the population.

**Overarching:** This synthesis is general compliance research, **not legal advice** — engage NY-licensed counsel to validate the UPL envelope (including the unresolved *Upsolve* posture and S7263 proprietor liability), the scrivener/selection-of-facts line, consent forms, SHIELD program, immigration data-handling, and SMS/TCPA flows before launch.
