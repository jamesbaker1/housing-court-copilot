**The open lane.** No tool today is a *full NYC eviction-case copilot for low-income tenants*. The market splits cleanly into (a) NYC-specific but narrow/rules-based (Roxanne = repairs only; JustFix = eligibility + letters; NY Courts DIY = static print forms) and (b) AI-powered with pleading upload but non-NY/general/paid (Courtroom5, Gavel, the CA/IL JusticeBench tools). The genuine whitespace — and the defensible wedge given a large RTC representation gap — is a **NYC-specific, free, intake-and-assembly autopilot that ingests eviction papers, organizes the tenant's facts/evidence, assembles court-ready packets, and warm-hands-off to a funded provider** — not an "AI lawyer" that competes with the bar. **Caveat:** the wedge's "court-ready packet for the tenant to file" component must be reconciled with two structural facts surfaced in the build plan — pro se tenants are statutorily *exempt* from mandatory e-filing (so an "assisted e-filing funnel" is narrower than first assumed), and the funded-provider partner that anchors the UPL posture must be a Phase-0 dependency, not a later add-on.
